Chronic Traumatic Encephalopathy

Chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE) is a degenerative brain disease that is caused by repeated head injuries. It is commonly found in athletes who participate in contact sports such as football, hockey, and boxing, but it can also occur in individuals who have suffered multiple concussions or other traumatic brain injuries.

Symptoms

CTE is characterized by the buildup of an abnormal protein called tau in the brain, which can result in a range of symptoms including memory loss, confusion, depression, and dementia. These symptoms typically develop years or even decades after the head injuries occur, and can worsen over time.
